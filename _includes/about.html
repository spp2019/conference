Globalisation has allowed an increasing number of researchers to move across borders, but most policy decisions are still taken by governments at the national and local level. SPP-Italy aims at bridging this gap: how can emigrated scientists contribute to and participate in the public life of their country of origin? How can scientific knowledge be disseminated across borders, while also ensuring its trustworthiness and acceptability by society at large?

The symposium will invite Italian researchers working in the UK, and other researchers with an interest in Italian affairs, to meet with science communicators and policymakers from Italy.

The symposium will also be an opportunity for young researchers with an interest in these themes to present their work to a wider audience. We hope this will prompt young, talented scientists to explore the impact their own research can have on society and the contribution it can make to political debates and decisions.
